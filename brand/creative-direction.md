# Creative Direction - P-XEL Studio

## DNA Constraints

Non-negotiable elements carried forward:
- P-XEL logo (SVG, unchanged)
- Violet accent (#6608F9) as primary brand color
- Dark/black + white high-contrast foundation
- Geometric, futuristic atmosphere
- Premium, minimal visual language
- No Inter, Arial, Roboto, Poppins
- No cliche purple SaaS gradients
- No cookie-cutter startup layouts
- No generic AI aesthetics (no neon meshes, no floating orbs, no gradient blobs)

---

## Direction 1: Architect Noir

### Concept
Precision engineering meets dark luxury. The site feels like a high-end architectural portfolio: stark contrasts, rigid grids, restrained type, and deliberate negative space. Every element is placed with intent. Nothing decorative. Nothing accidental.

### Strategic Angle
Positions P-XEL as a serious, methodical product builder. Appeals to PME directors and founders who want confidence, not flash. The restraint signals maturity and 16 years of experience.

### Typography Pairing
- **Headings:** Space Grotesk (geometric sans-serif, sharp terminals, distinctive "a" and "g", technical feel without being cold)
- **Body:** Satoshi (clean, modern, highly legible, warmer than Inter but still structured)
- **Mono accent:** JetBrains Mono (for stats, labels, technical details - signals code/build capability)

### Color Logic
- **Primary surface:** #0A0A0F (near-black, deeper than current #191D28)
- **Secondary surface:** #14141A (subtle elevation on dark)
- **Accent:** #6608F9 (violet, used sparingly - only CTAs, active states, key highlights)
- **Accent muted:** #6608F9 at 15% opacity (for borders, hover backgrounds)
- **Text primary:** #F5F5F7 (warm white on dark)
- **Text secondary:** #8A8A9A (muted lavender-gray)
- **Light sections:** #FAFAFA background, #111111 text (for blog, FAQ, readable sections)
- **Rule:** Violet never used as a background fill. Only as lines, dots, borders, text accents.

### Layout Logic
- 12-column grid, strict alignment
- Maximum content width: 1200px
- Generous vertical spacing (80-120px between sections)
- Asymmetric hero: headline left, visual/stat right
- Service cards: full-width rows with hover reveal (evolution of current pattern)
- Case studies: large image blocks with overlaid metadata
- No rounded corners except on small UI elements (buttons: 4px, cards: 0px)

### UI Density
Low. Lots of breathing room. One idea per viewport. Content is revealed through scroll, not packed into sections.

### Motion Language
- Page transitions: content fades in from bottom, 300ms, ease-out
- Scroll-triggered reveals: elements slide up 20px with opacity fade, staggered 100ms
- Hover states: underline grows from left, 200ms
- Stats: count up on scroll into view, no bounce
- No parallax. No floating elements. No particle effects.
- Cursor: default. No custom cursors.

### Background System
- Solid dark surfaces (#0A0A0F, #14141A)
- Subtle 1px horizontal rules to divide sections (violet or gray at 10% opacity)
- No gradients. No textures. No blurred shapes.
- Light sections for content-heavy pages (blog posts, FAQ)

### CTA Style
- Primary: Solid violet (#6608F9) background, white text, 4px radius, 48px height
- Secondary: Transparent, 1px violet border, violet text, 4px radius
- Hover: Primary darkens to #5506D4. Secondary fills with violet at 10% opacity.
- No shadows. No glow effects.

## Direction 2: Signal Studio

### Concept
Digital signal processing meets editorial design. Bold typography dominates. The layout borrows from high-end magazines and design annuals: oversized type, dramatic scale shifts, curated whitespace. The violet accent acts like a highlighter pen across a dark canvas.

### Strategic Angle
Positions P-XEL as a creative force that is also technical. Appeals to startup founders and agency partners who value design taste and distinctive output. The boldness signals confidence and creative authority.

### Typography Pairing
- **Headings:** Clash Display (strong geometric display face, sharp angles, high contrast between thick and thin strokes, unmistakable character)
- **Body:** General Sans (clean, slightly rounded, excellent readability, modern without being generic)
- **Accent/labels:** General Sans in caps with wide letter-spacing (0.12em) for service labels, navigation, metadata

### Color Logic
- **Primary surface:** #111116 (dark with slight warmth)
- **Secondary surface:** #1A1A22 (card backgrounds, elevated surfaces)
- **Accent:** #6608F9 (violet, used more expressively: large type accents, section markers, underlines)
- **Accent warm:** #F43F5E (rose, used only for results/stats/KPIs - signals performance and impact)
- **Text primary:** #FFFFFF (pure white on dark)
- **Text secondary:** #7B7B8E (cool gray)
- **Light sections:** #F8F7F4 (warm off-white), #1A1A22 text
- **Rule:** Violet can be used in large headline words (one word per headline max). Rose only for numbers and results.

### Layout Logic
- Fluid grid with dramatic scale contrasts
- Headlines span full width at large sizes (72-96px on desktop)
- Body text in narrower columns (max 680px) for readability
- Case studies: editorial spread layout (image left, text right, or full-bleed image with overlaid type)
- Service sections: large service name (72px+) with description below in smaller type
- Alternating dark/light sections create visual rhythm

### UI Density
Medium. Content is spaced generously but the large type and full-bleed images create visual impact without feeling empty.

### Motion Language
- Text reveals: words clip-mask in from bottom, staggered per word, 400ms, cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
- Image reveals: scale from 1.05 to 1.0 with opacity, 600ms
- Section transitions: horizontal rule grows from center, 500ms
- Hover on projects: image desaturates, title slides in
- Scroll progress indicator: thin violet line at top of viewport
- Ticker: horizontal scroll of service keywords (refined version of current pattern, slower, larger type)

### Background System
- Solid dark surfaces with occasional subtle noise texture (2% opacity, adds materiality)
- Light sections use warm off-white (#F8F7F4) to differentiate from sterile whites
- One decorative element allowed: a single thin violet diagonal line per page (geometric accent, not decoration)
- No blurs, no orbs, no gradients

### CTA Style
- Primary: Violet background, white text, 0px radius (sharp rectangle), 56px height, all-caps, letter-spacing 0.08em
- Secondary: White text, no border, underline on hover (editorial style)
- Hover: Primary shifts to white background with violet text (inversion)
- Scale: CTAs are large and confident. 18px text minimum.

## Direction 3: Carbon Grid

### Concept
Industrial precision meets Swiss design. A strict grid system with visible structural lines, monospaced metadata, and a technical color palette. The site feels like an interface designed by engineers who care about aesthetics. Every number is aligned. Every label is consistent. The grid is the design.

### Strategic Angle
Positions P-XEL as a builder who thinks in systems. Appeals to technical founders, product leads, and agencies who value structure and reliability. The systematic approach signals that P-XEL builds products with the same rigor it designs its own brand.

### Typography Pairing
- **Headings:** Instrument Sans (geometric, clean, slightly condensed, excellent at display sizes, distinctive without being showy)
- **Body:** Instrument Sans at regular weight (single-family system for consistency)
- **Mono:** IBM Plex Mono (for metadata, stats, dates, labels, process steps - signals technical precision)

### Color Logic
- **Primary surface:** #0C0C10 (deepest dark)
- **Secondary surface:** #161620 (grid cell backgrounds)
- **Grid lines:** #FFFFFF at 6% opacity (visible structure)
- **Accent:** #6608F9 (violet, used for active states, selected items, progress indicators)
- **Accent secondary:** #22C55E (green, used only for "shipped" / "live" / success states - signals delivery)
- **Text primary:** #E4E4E7 (slightly muted white)
- **Text secondary:** #71717A (zinc gray)
- **Light sections:** #FFFFFF background, #09090B text
- **Rule:** The grid lines are always visible as a structural element. Violet highlights what's active or important. Green marks delivered results.

### Layout Logic
- Visible 12-column grid with faint lines (#FFFFFF at 6%)
- Content aligns strictly to grid intersections
- Hero: headline in top-left quadrant, stats in top-right, CTA bottom-left
- Services: grid cards (3x1 or 2x2) with consistent padding
- Case studies: image fills grid cells, metadata in adjacent cell
- Process: horizontal timeline using grid columns
- All spacing derived from 8px base unit

### UI Density
Medium-high. Information is dense but organized. The grid structure prevents chaos. Feels like a well-organized dashboard, not a cluttered page.

### Motion Language
- Grid cells fade in on scroll, staggered by column (left to right), 200ms each
- Hover: grid cell border brightens from 6% to 20% opacity
- Active states: violet border appears on grid cell (2px, sharp)
- Stats: numbers tick up with monospace font (mechanical counter feel)
- No easing curves that feel bouncy. Linear or ease-out only.
- Page load: grid lines draw from top-left corner outward, 800ms

### Background System
- Dark surfaces with visible grid overlay (CSS grid lines at 6% opacity)
- Grid lines extend edge-to-edge (full viewport width)
- No texture, no noise, no blurs
- Light sections: white background with same grid overlay at 4% opacity
- The grid itself is the decorative system

### CTA Style
- Primary: Violet background, white text, 0px radius, 44px height, monospace label
- Secondary: Transparent, 1px white border at 20% opacity, white text
- Hover: Primary adds 1px white border. Secondary fills with white at 5%.
- Tertiary: Text link with arrow (->), monospace

## Final Recommendation

### Direction 1: Architect Noir

**Why this direction wins:**

1. **Most aligned with positioning.** "On conçoit. On construit. On livre." demands a visual language of precision and restraint, not creative showiness. Architect Noir communicates exactly this: methodical, confident, premium.

2. **Best for conversion.** The low UI density and clear hierarchy make CTAs unmistakable. The restrained palette ensures violet accents carry maximum visual weight where it matters: on booking buttons and key highlights.

3. **Strongest for SEO pages.** Service pages, city pages, and blog posts need to be readable and functional. Architect Noir's light-section mode and clean typography work perfectly for content-heavy pages without sacrificing the premium dark aesthetic on the homepage.

4. **Most sustainable.** The strict grid, limited color palette, and restrained motion are easier to maintain across 28+ pages. Signal Studio's editorial boldness could become inconsistent at scale. Carbon Grid's visible grid lines could feel limiting on content pages.

5. **Best typography match.** Space Grotesk + Satoshi + JetBrains Mono is the strongest pairing: distinctive enough to avoid blandness, professional enough for PME clients, and technical enough to signal build capability. The mono accent is a subtle differentiator that no competitor in the Belgian market uses.

6. **Avoids all pitfalls.** No generic AI meshes. No purple SaaS gradients. No startup cliches. No bouncy animations. The design feels like it belongs to a studio that has been doing this for 16 years.

**What to borrow from the other directions:**
- From Signal Studio: the idea of using violet on a single headline word for emphasis (use sparingly, only on homepage hero)
- From Carbon Grid: the monospace metadata pattern (already included via JetBrains Mono in Architect Noir)
- From Signal Studio: the warm off-white (#F8F7F4) for light sections instead of pure #FAFAFA (warmer, less sterile)
