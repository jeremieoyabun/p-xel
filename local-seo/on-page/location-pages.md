# Location Pages Strategy - P-XEL Studio

## Overview

Each city page serves as a local landing page that ranks for "[service] + [city]" queries, builds local authority, and drives bookings from that geographic area. Pages must be genuinely different, not just city name swaps.

## Page Template Structure

### Section 1: Hero

**Purpose:** Capture attention, state the value proposition with local context.

**Content elements:**
- H1 with city name and primary service positioning
- 1-2 sentence value proposition localized to the city
- Primary CTA: "Reservez un appel" (Calendly link)
- Secondary CTA: "Voir nos projets"
- No generic stock imagery. Use geometric/abstract P-XEL brand visuals.

**H1 templates:**

| City | H1 |
|---|---|
| Liege | Studio design produit a Liege. Du concept au produit livre. |
| Bruxelles | Votre partenaire design produit a Bruxelles. Conception, developpement, lancement. |
| Namur | Design produit et creation web a Namur. Rapide, premium, efficace. |
| Luxembourg | Studio design produit pour le Luxembourg. Apps, sites, identites. |

### Section 2: Services Overview

**Purpose:** Show what P-XEL delivers, with local framing.

**Content elements:**
- 4-6 service cards (sites web, applications, MVP, landing pages, UI/UX, branding)
- Each card: service name, 2-line description, link to service page
- One sentence contextualizing services for the local market

**Example for Bruxelles:**
> Les startups et scale-ups bruxelloises ont besoin d'un partenaire qui livre vite. Nous concevons et developpons votre produit digital de A a Z.

**Example for Namur:**
> PME namuroises, independants, structures publiques : nous creons des outils digitaux qui servent votre activite. Pas du design pour le design.

### Section 3: Local Context

**Purpose:** Prove knowledge of the local market. This is what makes each page unique.

**Content elements:**
- 2-3 paragraphs about the city's business and tech landscape
- Mention of specific local realities (business hubs, coworking spaces, economic sectors)
- How P-XEL fits into this ecosystem

**Local context per city:**

#### Liege
- Reference the WSL (Wallonia Science Lab), La Meuse tech corridor, Liege Science Park, SPI (agence de developpement economique)
- Mention the university ecosystem (ULiege spin-offs, student startups)
- Reference specific sectors: logistics (Liege Airport/TNT), biotech, gaming (studios around Liege)
- Mention LEANSQUARE, Noshaq as investment ecosystem
- Reference the ongoing urban renewal (Guillemins quarter, Baviere, Mediacity)

#### Bruxelles
- Reference the startup hub density: BeCentral, Co.Station, Silversquare, Start.Lac
- Mention EU institutional market (NGOs, associations, European institutions needing digital tools)
- Reference Molengeek, Becode for tech talent ecosystem
- Mention the bilingual reality: many Brussels companies need FR + NL + EN digital presence
- Reference the scale-up scene: fundraising-stage companies needing to professionalize their product fast

#### Namur
- Reference BEP (Bureau Economique de la Province), TRAKK (creative hub)
- Mention the public sector density (SPW, Walloon government institutions)
- Reference the growing independent/freelance community
- Mention proximity to Liege (30 min) as an advantage for in-person collaboration
- Reference local business events: Kikk Festival (digital creativity), Forward

#### Luxembourg
- Distinguish between Province de Luxembourg (BE) and Grand-Duche de Luxembourg
- Reference the cross-border economy: many Belgian businesses serve Luxembourg clients
- Mention the financial sector (fintech opportunities)
- Reference Luxembourg's high digital adoption and purchasing power
- Mention multilingual needs: FR + DE + EN + LU
- Reference specific hubs: Technoport (Esch), Luxembourg House of Financial Technology

### Section 4: Selected Projects

**Purpose:** Show relevant work, ideally with local connection.

**Content elements:**
- 2-3 project cards with visuals
- For each: client industry, what was delivered, result/impact
- If possible, feature projects from or relevant to that city
- If no city-specific projects yet, show projects from similar industries present in that city

**Fallback approach for cities without local clients:**
> Nous n'avons pas encore de client base a [city], mais nous avons livre des projets similaires pour des entreprises dans [same sector as city's economy].

### Section 5: Process

**Purpose:** Make working with P-XEL feel simple and clear.

**Content elements:**
- 3-4 step process (Appel decouverte > Proposition > Design + Dev > Livraison)
- Emphasize speed: "Votre site en 4 semaines. Votre MVP en 6 a 8 semaines."
- Mention how remote collaboration works for non-Liege clients
- For Liege page: mention in-person option

### Section 7: CTA

**Purpose:** Convert the visitor.

**Content elements:**
- Strong headline: "Pret a lancer votre projet a [city] ?"
- 1 sentence reinforcing the value proposition
- Calendly booking button as primary CTA
- "Ou envoyez-nous un email a hello@p-xel.be" as secondary
- Trust signal: "Appel gratuit de 30 minutes. Sans engagement."

## Internal Linking From City Pages

### Links going out from each city page

- Link to each relevant service page: /services/creation-site-web/, /services/application-web/, /services/mvp/, /services/landing-page/, /services/ui-ux/, /services/branding/
- Link to /portfolio/ or specific case studies
- Link to /a-propos/ (for trust)
- Link to /contact/ (secondary to Calendly CTA)
- Link to /blog/ articles relevant to the city or its industries

### Links coming into city pages

- From service pages: "Decouvrez nos services a Liege" or "Nous intervenons aussi a Bruxelles"
- From blog posts mentioning a city or its business context
- From the homepage: city links in a "Ou nous intervenons" section
- From the footer: list of city pages
- From other city pages: "Nous sommes aussi presents a [other city]"

## Service + City Combination Pages (Phase 2)

### Criteria for creation

Create a /services/[service]-[city]/ page only when:

1. The "[service] + [city]" query shows 40+ monthly searches in Google Keyword Planner
2. The existing city page does not rank in the top 10 for that combination
3. The existing service page does not rank in the top 10 for that combination
4. There is enough unique content to justify a separate page (minimum 800 words of non-duplicated content)

### Likely Phase 2 candidates

| Page | Target keyword | Estimated volume |
|---|---|---|
| /services/creation-site-web-liege/ | creation site web Liege | 70-150 |
| /services/creation-site-web-bruxelles/ | creation site web Bruxelles | 100-200 |
| /services/agence-branding-liege/ | agence branding Liege | 30-50 |
| /services/application-web-bruxelles/ | developpeur application Bruxelles | 50-100 |

### What NOT to create

- Do not create service + city pages for every combination. Only for validated demand.
- Do not create pages for cities where P-XEL has no credibility or project history unless search volume justifies it.
- Do not create thin pages that are just the service page with a city name inserted.

## Differentiation Checklist

Before publishing a city page, verify:

- [ ] Local context section mentions at least 3 specific local references (organizations, hubs, events, sectors)
- [ ] FAQ questions are unique to this city, not copy-pasted from other city pages
- [ ] At least one project or case study has a connection to the city's economy
- [ ] The meta description is unique and not a template with swapped city names
- [ ] The H1 is unique and not just "[Service] a [City]"
- [ ] Internal links from this page go to at least 3 service pages
- [ ] Schema markup includes the city in areaServed
- [ ] The page passes a "would this help someone in [city] specifically?" test
