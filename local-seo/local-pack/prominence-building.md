# Prominence Building - Local Authority for P-XEL

## What Drives Prominence for a Small Studio

Prominence is Google's measure of how well-known and trusted a business is. For a small studio like P-XEL competing against larger agencies, prominence is built through:

1. Review volume and quality
2. Citation consistency across the web
3. Backlinks from relevant, authoritative sources
4. Brand mentions (even without links)
5. Directory presence on industry platforms
6. Engagement signals on GBP

Prominence is the factor P-XEL can actively build over time. Unlike proximity (fixed) and relevance (optimized once), prominence compounds.

## Review Volume and Velocity Targets

### Current baseline

Audit current review count on GBP. Most Belgian web design studios have between 5 and 30 reviews. Reaching 20+ quality reviews puts P-XEL in the top tier for Liege.

### Targets

| Milestone | Timeline | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation | Month 1-3 | Reach 10 reviews |
| Competitive | Month 4-6 | Reach 20 reviews |
| Dominant | Month 7-12 | Reach 30+ reviews |
| Maintenance | Ongoing | 2-3 new reviews per month |

### How to get reviews

1. **Post-project request:** After every project delivery, send a personalized email with a direct link to the GBP review page. Do this within 48 hours of project completion while satisfaction is fresh.

2. **Review link:** Create a short URL (p-xel.be/avis) that redirects to the GBP review form. Include this in email signatures and follow-up emails.

3. **Timing script:**
   - Day 0: Project delivered
   - Day 2: Send thank-you email with review request
   - Day 7: If no review, send a gentle reminder
   - Day 14: Final reminder, then stop

4. **What to ask for:** Request that clients mention the specific service ("site web," "application," "branding") and their city. This adds keyword relevance to the review. Do not script the review, but suggest: "Si vous pouviez mentionner le type de projet et votre ville, ca nous aiderait beaucoup."

5. **Past clients:** Reach out to all past satisfied clients. Even projects from 2-3 years ago. A batch of 5-8 reviews from past clients creates immediate momentum.

### Review response protocol

- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Thank the client by name
- Mention the service delivered ("Merci pour votre confiance pour la refonte de votre site web")
- Keep responses genuine, not templated
- For negative reviews: acknowledge, offer to resolve offline, stay professional

## Citation Consistency Score

Citations are mentions of P-XEL's business name, address, and phone number (NAP) across the web. Consistency matters more than volume.

### NAP format to use everywhere

```
P-XEL Studio
[Street address], 4000 Liege, Belgium
+32 [phone number]
p-xel.be
```

Use this exact format on every platform. No variations ("P-XEL" vs "PXEL" vs "P-Xel Studio" vs "P-XEL Design"). Inconsistency confuses Google and weakens prominence.

## Brand Mentions Strategy

Unlinked brand mentions still contribute to prominence. Google recognizes "P-XEL Studio" as an entity whether or not it links to the website.

### How to generate brand mentions

1. **Social media activity:** Post regularly on LinkedIn (primary for B2B in Belgium), Twitter/X, and Instagram. Each post mentioning P-XEL is indexed.

2. **Podcast appearances:** Belgian business and tech podcasts. Even small podcasts generate show notes with brand mentions.
   - Targets: Startups.be podcast, Les Technos (RTBF), Digimedia events

3. **Conference talks:** Present at WordCamp Belgium, Liege Creative, ULiege entrepreneurship events. Speaker bios and schedules mention the brand.

4. **Blog comments and forum participation:** Meaningful contributions on Belgian tech forums, Reddit Belgium (/r/Belgium, /r/Wallonia), and design communities.

5. **Client testimonials on third-party sites:** When clients write about their experience on their own blog or social media, it generates brand mentions.

## Awards and Directory Features

### Industry directories (high impact)

| Platform | Action | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Sortlist | Create a complete profile, collect reviews, bid on projects | High (major source of Belgian leads) |
| Clutch | Create profile, collect verified reviews (minimum 3) | High (international credibility) |
| Awwwards | Submit best project for Site of the Day consideration | High (design credibility, backlink) |
| CSS Design Awards | Submit projects | Medium (easier to win than Awwwards) |
| The FWA | Submit projects | Medium |
| Behance | Showcase all projects | Medium (portfolio visibility) |
| Dribbble | Active posting, Pro account | Medium |
| ProductHunt | Launch any tools or side projects | Medium |

### Awards strategy

1. **Awwwards:** Requires a truly exceptional project. Budget for at least one portfolio piece built specifically to award-winning standards. This is a long-term play but the backlink and credibility are worth it.

2. **CSS Design Awards:** More accessible. Submit 2-3 best projects. Nomination alone adds credibility.

3. **Sortlist Awards:** Sortlist ranks agencies in Belgium. Getting into their "Top Web Design Agencies in Belgium" list is achievable with 5+ verified reviews and a complete profile.

4. **Local awards:** AWEX export awards, Digital Wallonia awards, Liege Creative awards. Less competitive, strong local signal.

### Timeline for directory setup

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| Month 1 | Sortlist profile (complete, optimized), Clutch profile, Behance portfolio |
| Month 2 | Dribbble Pro, CSS Design Awards submission, Malt profile |
| Month 3 | First Clutch review collected, Sortlist reviews requested |
| Month 4 | Awwwards submission (if portfolio piece is ready) |
| Month 6 | Review progress, fill gaps in directory coverage |

## Social Proof Amplification

### How to turn existing proof into prominence signals

1. **Client logos on homepage:** Display logos of recognizable clients. This builds trust for visitors and gets indexed by Google as entity associations.

2. **Testimonial schema markup:** Add Review schema to testimonials on the website. Google may display star ratings in search results.

3. **LinkedIn recommendations:** Request LinkedIn recommendations from clients. These are indexed and contribute to brand prominence.

4. **Case study distribution:** When publishing a case study, share it on LinkedIn, submit it to design galleries, and email it to relevant Belgian journalists.

5. **Review snippets in GBP posts:** When you get a great review, create a GBP post highlighting it (with permission).

### Monthly prominence checklist

- [ ] Requested reviews from completed projects
- [ ] Responded to all new reviews
- [ ] Published at least 4 GBP posts
- [ ] Published one piece of content (blog, case study, or guest article)
- [ ] Made one outreach for a backlink or brand mention
- [ ] Checked citation consistency on top 5 platforms
- [ ] Updated Sortlist/Clutch profiles if new projects completed
- [ ] Shared one project or insight on LinkedIn
