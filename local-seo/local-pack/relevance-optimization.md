# Relevance Optimization - GBP and Website Alignment

## Category Optimization in Google Business Profile

The primary category is the single most important ranking signal in GBP. Secondary categories add breadth.

### Primary category recommendation

**Web Designer** - This is the most accurate category for P-XEL and matches the highest-volume search queries in Belgium ("web designer Liege," "web designer Belgique").

### Secondary categories to add

Add these in order of relevance:

1. **Graphic Designer** - Covers branding and visual identity queries
2. **Software Company** - Covers web app and MVP development queries
3. **Internet Marketing Service** - Covers digital strategy queries
4. **Mobile App Developer** - Covers app design and development queries
5. **UX Designer** - If available in your GBP region (not always available)

### Categories to avoid

- "Advertising Agency" - Wrong positioning, attracts wrong clients
- "Marketing Consultant" - Too broad, low relevance
- "Computer Consultant" - Attracts IT support queries

### How to verify category choices

1. Search "web designer Liege" on Google
2. Click on each business in the local pack
3. Note their primary and secondary categories
4. Match or differentiate strategically

## How Website Content Reinforces GBP Relevance

Google cross-references GBP information with website content. Misalignment weakens ranking. Alignment strengthens it.

### Alignment checklist

- [ ] Website title tag includes "Web Designer" and "Liege"
- [ ] Homepage H1 contains the primary service and location
- [ ] About page mentions Liege, Belgium, and the full range of services
- [ ] Each service listed in GBP has a corresponding dedicated page on the website
- [ ] NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on website footer matches GBP exactly
- [ ] Website uses the same business name as GBP (P-XEL Studio, not variations)
- [ ] Schema markup on the website matches GBP categories and services

### Content reinforcement strategy

For every service in GBP, the website must have:
1. A dedicated service page with the service name in the H1
2. At least one case study or project showcasing that service
3. A mention of that service on the homepage
4. Internal links between service pages

This tells Google: "The GBP listing and the website are about the same things."

## GBP Post Content Strategy

GBP posts expire after 7 days (events last until end date). Posting weekly keeps the profile active and sends freshness signals.

### Weekly posting schedule

| Week | Post type | Content theme |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | What's New | New project launched (with screenshot) |
| Week 2 | Offer | Free discovery call CTA |
| Week 3 | What's New | Design insight or tip related to a service |
| Week 4 | Event | Workshop, meetup, or webinar (even virtual) |

### Post content guidelines

- Always include a high-quality image (1200x900 px minimum)
- Always include a CTA button ("Book online," "Learn more," "Call now")
- Include target keywords naturally ("site web Liege," "design UI/UX," "application web")
- Keep text under 300 words, front-load the value
- Link to relevant service or portfolio page on the website
- Use French for all posts (primary market)

### Post examples

**Project launch post:**
"Nouveau projet livre : refonte complete du site web pour [Client Name]. Design sur mesure, performance optimisee et SEO integre. Resultat : un site rapide, clair et qui convertit. Vous avez un projet similaire ? Reservez un appel decouverte."

**Service highlight post:**
"Vous lancez un produit digital ? Notre accompagnement MVP couvre tout : strategie produit, design UI/UX, developpement et lancement. De l'idee a la mise en ligne en quelques semaines. Basee a Liege, P-XEL Studio accompagne startups et PME dans toute la Belgique."

## How Case Studies Reinforce Service Relevance

Case studies are the strongest bridge between GBP relevance and website authority. Each case study should:

### Structure for maximum relevance

1. **Title:** Include service + client industry + location when possible
   - "Refonte de site web pour une fintech a Bruxelles"
   - "MVP design et developpement pour une startup liégeoise"

2. **Content must mention:**
   - The specific service delivered (matching GBP service names)
   - The client's location (reinforces geographic relevance)
   - The problem solved (matches search intent)
   - Measurable results (conversion rate, load time, launch speed)

3. **Schema markup:** Use Case Study or CreativeWork schema with mentions of services and locations

4. **Internal linking:** Each case study links to the corresponding service page, and vice versa

### Minimum case studies needed per service

| Service | Target case studies | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Creation de site web | 3 | High |
| Design UI/UX | 2 | High |
| Application web / MVP | 2 | High |
| Branding | 2 | Medium |
| Landing page | 1 | Medium |
| Refonte de site | 1 | Medium |

If real case studies are limited, use detailed project breakdowns of existing work. Never fabricate clients or results.
