# Proximity Signals - Local Pack Strategy for P-XEL

## How Proximity Affects Local Pack Ranking

Google uses three factors for local pack ranking: relevance, distance, and prominence. Distance (proximity) is the one P-XEL cannot directly control, but can strategically work around.

When someone searches "web designer Liege" from the center of Liege, Google measures the distance between the searcher and every business that matches. Closer businesses get a ranking boost. This is automatic and non-negotiable.

Key fact: proximity weight varies by query type. For "web designer near me," proximity is heavily weighted. For "meilleur studio web Liege," relevance and prominence carry more weight because the user specified a city rather than requesting nearby results.

## Liege as the Base: Radius of Influence

P-XEL operates from Liege. Without any optimization, the natural radius of influence in the local pack is roughly 5-10 km from the registered address. Beyond that, rankings drop unless prominence and relevance signals compensate.

### Natural ranking zones from Liege

| Zone | Radius | Expected local pack visibility |
|------|--------|-------------------------------|
| Liege center | 0-3 km | Strong (default advantage) |
| Greater Liege (Herstal, Seraing, Angleur, Chenee) | 3-10 km | Moderate to strong with optimization |

## Ranking in Nearby Cities Without Physical Offices

P-XEL does not need a physical office in Brussels or Namur to appear in local results there. The strategy depends on the business type chosen in Google Business Profile.

### Service Area Business (SAB) Approach

Setting up as a Service Area Business allows P-XEL to define service areas without showing a physical address. This is the correct setup for a studio that serves clients across Belgium remotely.

**How to configure:**
1. In GBP, set business type to "Service area business"
2. Define service areas: Liege, Brussels, Namur, Charleroi, Luxembourg (city)
3. The physical address remains Liege but is hidden from the public listing
4. Google will consider P-XEL for queries in all defined service areas

**Limitation:** SAB listings still rank weaker in distant cities compared to businesses physically located there. Proximity still matters, but the SAB designation tells Google you serve those areas.

### Organic + Local Hybrid Strategy for Distant Cities

For Brussels and Luxembourg, where proximity disadvantage is significant:
- Build dedicated service + city landing pages on the website (e.g., /services/web-design-bruxelles)
- These pages rank in organic results even when the local pack is dominated by Brussels-based studios
- Use these pages to capture traffic that the local pack cannot deliver

## Service Area Business vs Storefront

| Factor | SAB (recommended for P-XEL) | Storefront |
|--------|------------------------------|------------|
| Address shown publicly | No | Yes |
| Service area defined | Yes (multiple cities) | Limited to immediate area |
| "Get directions" button | No | Yes |
| Works for remote/hybrid studios | Yes | Not ideal |
| Proximity boost in home city | Same | Same |
| Ranking in distant cities | Better (service areas respected) | Worse (single location bias) |

**Decision: P-XEL should use the SAB model.** The studio does not receive walk-in clients. Clients book calls and work remotely. SAB is the correct classification.

## Address Strategy

### Options evaluated

1. **Home/registered address in Liege (current):** Acceptable. Many successful agencies use this. The address is hidden in SAB mode. GBP verification happens at this address.

2. **Coworking space in Liege (e.g., La Grand Poste, MakerHub):** Better for credibility if sharing the address publicly. Useful if switching to storefront mode. Some coworking spaces allow GBP registration, others do not. Verify before committing.

3. **Virtual office:** Risky. Google actively suspends listings tied to virtual office addresses, especially well-known ones (Regus, etc.). Not recommended.

4. **Second address in Brussels:** Only if P-XEL eventually has a real, staffed presence there. A fake second location will result in suspension. Do not do this.

**Recommendation:** Keep the current Liege address. Use SAB mode. If a Brussels presence becomes real (even part-time coworking), add a second GBP listing at that point.

## Impact of Searcher Location on Results

The local pack a user sees depends heavily on where they are physically standing when they search.

- A user in Liege center searching "web designer" will see Liege-based businesses
- The same user driving through Namur searching "web designer" will see Namur-based businesses
- A user searching "web designer Liege" from Brussels will see Liege businesses, but proximity still slightly favors any Liege business closer to the searcher's general direction

This means:
- P-XEL will naturally dominate for searchers physically in Liege
- For searchers in other cities, P-XEL needs strong relevance and prominence to compensate for distance
- City-specific queries ("web designer Liege") are more controllable than generic queries ("web designer near me")

**SEO implication:** Target city-qualified keywords in content strategy. "Web designer Liege" is more valuable than "web designer near me" because it works regardless of searcher location.

## Practical: Competing in Brussels and Luxembourg from Liege

### Brussels strategy

Brussels is the highest-value market in Belgium for web design services. P-XEL is 100 km away. Here is the realistic plan:

1. **GBP service area:** Include Brussels Capital Region in service areas
2. **Dedicated landing page:** /services/web-design-bruxelles with Brussels-specific content, case studies from Brussels clients, references to Brussels business context
3. **Brussels-specific blog content:** "Comment choisir un studio web a Bruxelles" - rank organically for Brussels queries
4. **Brussels citations:** List P-XEL on Brussels business directories (hub.brussels, 1819.brussels)
5. **Brussels client reviews:** When a Brussels client leaves a review, they often mention their city. This reinforces Brussels relevance in GBP
6. **Realistic expectation:** P-XEL will not consistently appear in the Brussels local 3-pack against established Brussels agencies. The organic strategy (landing pages, blog) is more reliable for capturing Brussels traffic

### Luxembourg strategy

Luxembourg is a high-value, low-competition market for French-speaking web design:

1. **GBP service area:** Include Luxembourg (city) in service areas
2. **Dedicated landing page:** /services/web-design-luxembourg
3. **Angle:** Position as a Belgian studio serving Luxembourg clients at better value than Luxembourg agencies (Luxembourg labor costs are higher)
4. **Citations:** List on editus.lu, yellow.lu
5. **Language:** Content in French works perfectly for Luxembourg market
6. **Realistic expectation:** Lower competition means better chances of local pack visibility even from Liege

### Priority order

1. Liege and greater Liege (immediate, highest conversion probability)
2. Liege province cities (Verviers, Huy, Waremme - medium effort, decent return)
3. Brussels (high effort, high value, organic-first approach)
4. Namur and Charleroi (medium effort, moderate value)
5. Luxembourg (medium effort, high value per client)
