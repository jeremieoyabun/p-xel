# Perspectives - Article Audit (v2)

Ruthless audit of the 6 legacy articles. Each evaluated against one question: does this article have a realistic path to generating qualified leads for P-XEL?

Evaluation criteria:
1. **Keyword opportunity**: Is there real search volume? Is the keyword winnable?
2. **Commercial proximity**: How close is the searcher to buying?
3. **Uniqueness**: Does P-XEL have a distinctive angle, or is this commodity content?
4. **Rewrite effort vs. return**: Is the ROI of rewriting this article justified?
5. **Cannibalization risk**: Does this compete with service pages or other articles?

---

## 1. Combien coute un site web en 2026

- **Source**: `combien-coute-un-site-web-en-2026.md`
- **Legacy date**: Nov 2025
- **Hero image**: Article-large_Web-design.webp

### Keyword analysis
- **Target**: combien coute un site web
- **Francophone volume**: 5,000-10,000/month (France + Belgium + Switzerland + Luxembourg)
- **Belgian volume**: 300-600/month
- **Competition**: Medium-High. Many articles exist, but most are shallow listicles or outdated.
- **Winnability**: High. A comprehensive, updated, well-structured article with FAQ schema and real pricing data can outrank generic content. P-XEL's own pricing on the service page adds E-E-A-T credibility.

### Commercial proximity
Very high. People searching "combien coute un site web" are in active buying mode. They have a project in mind. They need a price reference before contacting providers. The path from this article to a booked call is: read price ranges, realize P-XEL's prices are clear, click CTA, book.

### Legacy content quality
The existing article has 4 pricing tiers with ranges. Structurally sound but:
- Price ranges are from the old site (landing 900-3500, vitrine 3500-10000, e-commerce 6000-30000, web app 20000+). These need rechecking against P-XEL's current service page prices (landing 1500+, vitrine 5000+, e-commerce 8000+).
- Missing: refonte pricing (the keyword "prix refonte site web" alone is 300-600/month)
- Missing: freelance vs agency vs studio price comparison
- Missing: what is included vs. what costs extra
- Missing: FAQ schema
- No P-XEL differentiation or CTA

### What must change in the rewrite
1. Align prices with service page (landing 1500+, vitrine 5000+, e-commerce 8000+, web app 8000-50000+)
2. Add H2 section on refonte pricing (captures "prix refonte site web" cluster)
3. Add comparison table: freelance vs agency vs studio pricing model
4. Add "what is included at each price point" (P-XEL's deliverables as the benchmark)
5. Add section: "pourquoi les prix varient autant" (covers the long-tail)
6. Add FAQ schema (min 5 questions targeting long-tail pricing queries)
7. Add mid-article CTA to service page, end-article CTA to contact/Calendly
8. Cross-link to aides-digitalisation (reduce your cost), no-code-vs-code (budget depends on tech choice)
9. Use neutral French (not Belgian-specific) to capture francophone-wide traffic
10. Update title to drop the year OR keep it and commit to annual refresh

### Verdict: KEEP. HEAVY REWRITE. PRIORITY 1.
This is the single most valuable article P-XEL can publish. It alone can drive 500-1,500 monthly visits with strong commercial intent across all francophone markets. Non-negotiable.

Covers two programs:

- Missing: how P-XEL specifically helps with the application
- Missing: FAQ schema
- Missing: cross-link to pricing article

1. Verify all subsidy amounts, eligibility, and conditions are current for 2026
2. Add clear section: "Comment P-XEL vous accompagne dans la demande de prime"

5. Cross-link to contact/Calendly ("we handle the paperwork")
6. Add a brief mention of Luxembourg subsidies with link to /luxembourg/ city page

### Verdict: KEEP. MEDIUM REWRITE. PRIORITY 2.
Unique competitive advantage content. No competitor covers this well. Low competition, high conversion potential. The subsidy angle directly reduces buying friction for P-XEL's services.

## 3. No-code, low-code ou full-code

- **Source**: `no-code.md`
- **Legacy date**: Dec 2025
- **Hero image**: Article-large_Code.webp
- **Thumbnail**: Articles-No-code.webp

### Keyword analysis
- **Target**: no-code vs code
- **Francophone volume**: 1,500-3,000/month
- **Belgian volume**: 80-200/month
- **Competition**: Medium. Many articles exist but few from actual studios who build with both approaches.
- **Winnability**: High. P-XEL's credibility as a studio that works with both no-code and full-code gives a genuine authority angle.

### Commercial proximity
Medium-High. Searchers are evaluating approaches before choosing a provider. They are one step before "hire someone." The natural conversion path: read comparison, realize they need expert guidance, contact P-XEL.

### Legacy content quality
Covers Webflow, Bubble, Retool, Appsmith vs full-code. Has a decision framework. But:
- Too neutral. Reads like a Wikipedia article, not a studio's perspective.
- No P-XEL opinion on when to use what
- No real project examples
- Some tool references may be outdated
- No FAQ schema

### What must change in the rewrite
1. Add strong P-XEL opinion: "We recommend no-code when X, full-code when Y"
2. Add real examples (anonymized if needed): "For a client who needed X, we chose Y because Z"
3. Add decision matrix table (project type x complexity x budget x scale = recommendation)
4. Remove or update stale tool references
5. Add FAQ schema
6. Cross-link to services/application-web-mvp (for custom code projects)
7. Cross-link to combien-coute-un-site-web (budget implications of each choice)
8. Add end-article CTA: "Not sure which approach fits? Book a call."

### Verdict: KEEP. MEDIUM REWRITE. PRIORITY 3.
Solid decision-making content with francophone-wide reach. P-XEL can genuinely own this topic as a studio that works across the spectrum.

## 4. IA pour les designers : les vrais usages en 2026

- **Source**: `ia-pour-les-designers-les-vrais-usages-en-2026.md`
- **Legacy date**: Oct 2025
- **Hero image**: Article-large_AI.webp
- **Thumbnail**: Articles-AI.webp

### Keyword analysis
- **Target (legacy)**: ia pour les designers
- **Target (proposed reframe)**: ia creation produit digital
- **Francophone volume**: 100-300/month for the reframed keyword. "ia pour les designers" is ~200-400/month but targets the wrong audience.
- **Competition**: Low. Few French-language articles cover AI from a practical product-creation angle.
- **Winnability**: High (low competition), but low volume limits impact.

### Commercial proximity
Low-Medium. The legacy angle ("for designers") targets peers, not buyers. The reframed angle ("AI speeds up your project") is better but still informational. The path to conversion is indirect: read about AI workflow, be impressed by P-XEL's approach, maybe contact later.

### Legacy content quality
8 sections covering AI tools for ideation, prototyping, asset generation, copywriting, testing, personalization, design systems, workflow automation. Some sections are thin. Tool recommendations will be outdated by the time this publishes.

### The hard question: is this worth the effort?
- The reframe requires near-total rewrite (different audience, different angle, different structure)
- Volume is low (100-300/month at best)
- Conversion path is the weakest of the surviving articles
- BUT: it directly supports P-XEL's "AI Product Designer" positioning
- AND: the topic is growing. Volume will increase over time.
- AND: no competitor in the francophone studio space covers this well

### What must change in the rewrite
1. Complete angle change: from "tools for designers" to "how AI makes your project faster and cheaper"
2. Frame around client benefits: "Your MVP ships 40% faster because we use AI for X, Y, Z"
3. Remove outdated tool-by-tool reviews. Focus on workflows and outcomes.
4. Add concrete time/cost savings examples
5. Cross-link to services/application-web-mvp
6. Add FAQ schema
7. Add CTA: "Want an AI-accelerated project? Book a call."

### Verdict: KEEP. HEAVY REWRITE. PRIORITY 4.
Not a traffic play. A positioning play. Low volume but high brand alignment and zero competition. Publish after the top 3, and only if the rewrite is genuinely strong. A weak version of this article does more harm than good.

## 5. Pourquoi un design system est essentiel en 2026

- **Source**: `pourquoi-un-design-system-est-essentiel-en-2026.md`
- **Legacy date**: Oct 2025
- **Hero image**: Article-large_design-system.webp
- **Thumbnail**: Articles-Design-system.webp

### Keyword analysis
- **Target**: design system entreprise
- **Francophone volume**: 100-200/month for "design system entreprise". "Design system" alone is 1,000-2,000/month but dominated by English-language content (Google, Material Design, IBM).
- **Competition**: Very high for the head term, low for the French niche.
- **Winnability**: Uncertain. The French-language niche is small. Even if P-XEL ranks #1 for "design system entreprise," the traffic ceiling is ~50-100 visits/month.

### Commercial proximity
Low. People searching "design system" are typically designers or developers doing research. They are not looking to hire a studio. The very rare exceptions (enterprise product teams evaluating design system creation) represent maybe 5-10% of the search volume.

### Legacy content quality
10 sections. Most are generic benefits lists that any ChatGPT can produce. Several sections overlap (consistency + brand coherence, speed + cost reduction, scalability + future-proofing). No unique P-XEL perspective. No case studies. No concrete numbers.

### The hard question: is this worth the effort?
- Volume: ~100-200/month (French niche), dropping to ~50-100 realistic visits if ranking top 3
- Conversion: Very low. Maybe 1-2 leads per year from this article.
- Rewrite effort: Heavy (needs condensing from 10 to 5 sections, new angle, new examples)
- Opportunity cost: The time spent rewriting this article is better spent writing "Refonte de site web" (3,000-5,000/month, very high conversion) or "Combien coute une application web" (1,000-2,000/month, very high conversion)

### Verdict: CUT. DEFER TO PHASE 2 AT EARLIEST.
The math does not work. 50-100 monthly visits with low conversion potential does not justify a heavy rewrite. The Phase 2 article opportunities (refonte, app pricing, agence vs freelance) are 10-50x more valuable. If P-XEL wants design system content later, it should be a lightweight service page section, not a standalone article.

## Cannibalization analysis

### Risk 1: Pricing article vs. service page pricing section (MANAGED)
- **Article** owns: "combien coute un site web", "prix site web", market-wide pricing context
- **Service page** owns: "creation site web belgique", P-XEL's specific prices and deliverables
- **Resolution**: Different intent. Market guide vs. provider page. Cross-link naturally.

### Risk 2: Subsidies article vs. Luxembourg city page (NONE)
- **Article** owns: all "aides digitalisation" keywords for Belgium
- **Luxembourg page** owns: "agence web luxembourg" and local service queries
- **Resolution**: Article mentions Luxembourg subsidies briefly with link to city page. No keyword overlap.

### Risk 3: No-code article vs. app/MVP service page (LOW)
- **Article** owns: comparison and decision framework keywords
- **Service page** owns: "developpement application web belgique" (hire-us intent)
- **Resolution**: Article recommends; service page sells. Article links to service page as the "if you need custom code" destination.

### Risk 4: AI article vs. service pages mentioning AI (NONE)
- **Article** owns: "ia creation produit digital" topic cluster
- **Service pages** mention AI as a capability bullet point, not a topic
- **Resolution**: No overlap. Article is the canonical AI content; service pages defer to it.
