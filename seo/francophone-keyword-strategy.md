# Francophone Keyword Strategy - P-XEL Studio

## Strategic premise

P-XEL is a Belgian studio, but the French language market extends far beyond Belgium. For certain keyword families, P-XEL can capture traffic from France, Switzerland, and Luxembourg without any additional pages - simply by ranking for non-geolocalized queries.

This is not about becoming a French agency. It is about identifying which keywords travel across borders and which are locked to Belgian geography.

---

## Market segmentation

### Market 1: Belgium (primary, all keywords)
- FR-BE search behavior
- City-specific queries (Liege, Bruxelles, Namur)
- Belgian-specific terms (cheques entreprises, numero TVA, offre de prix)
- All service pages, city pages, and editorial content target this market

### Market 2: Luxembourg (secondary, strategic)
- High purchasing power per capita
- SME Package Digital subsidy creates a direct conversion hook
- French is a primary business language
- P-XEL already targets this with `/luxembourg/` city page
- Keywords: "agence web luxembourg", "creation site web luxembourg", "aides digitalisation luxembourg"

### Market 3: Northern France (opportunistic, editorial only)
- Lille, Metz, Strasbourg metro areas are geographically close to Liege
- Service pages will not rank for French cities (no local presence, no GBP)
- But editorial content (pricing, guides, comparisons) ranks nationally
- P-XEL can capture French traffic through perspectives articles without claiming local presence

### Market 4: Switzerland (opportunistic, editorial only)
- French-speaking Switzerland (Romandie: Geneva, Lausanne, Fribourg)
- Very high purchasing power
- Similar search behavior to Belgian French
- Same approach as France: editorial content captures traffic, service pages do not target

## Content approach by market reach

### For Belgium-only pages
- Use Belgian French naturally (offre de prix, numero d'entreprise, etc.)
- Reference Belgian cities, provinces, and regions
- Mention Belgian subsidies where relevant
- LocalBusiness schema with Belgian address
- Internal links to city pages

### For francophone-wide pages
- Use neutral French (avoid belgicisms that French readers would not understand)
- Do not reference Belgian-specific context unless it adds value
- Use EUR pricing (shared currency with France, Luxembourg)
- Mention CHF equivalent only if Swiss traffic becomes significant (track first)
- Do not claim presence in cities where P-XEL has no office
- Add Belgian authority signals subtly: "P-XEL Studio, base a Liege" in author box, not in every paragraph
- Let the content quality and keyword targeting do the ranking work

### For Luxembourg-specific pages
- Reference SME Package Digital and other Luxembourg subsidies
- Mention cross-border proximity (Liege to Luxembourg: 1h30)
- Use Luxembourg business context where appropriate
- Dedicated city page (`/luxembourg/`) already planned

## Tracking plan

### Belgium metrics
- Google Search Console: filter by country = Belgium
- Track impressions, clicks, CTR, position for all primary keywords
- Monthly city-level ranking checks for Liege, Bruxelles, Namur, Luxembourg

### Francophone metrics
- Google Search Console: filter by country = France, Switzerland, Luxembourg separately
- Track which perspectives articles attract non-Belgian traffic
- Monitor if non-Belgian traffic converts (UTM on CTAs, Calendly tracking)
- If French traffic converts at >1%, consider adding "Nous travaillons aussi avec des clients en France" to relevant pages

### Quarterly review
- Reassess keyword targets based on actual ranking and CTR data
- Identify new keyword opportunities from Search Console "queries you rank for but don't target"
- Kill or consolidate underperforming pages
- Double down on pages that show traction (add internal links, update content, improve CTAs)
