# Perspectives - Editorial SEO Strategy

## Purpose

/perspectives/ is P-XEL's editorial section. It serves three goals:
1. Capture high-intent organic traffic in French-speaking Belgium (and broader francophone market)
2. Build topical authority around P-XEL's core services
3. Convert readers into booked discovery calls

This is not a blog. It is a lead-generation engine dressed as editorial content.

## Naming

"Perspectives" instead of "Blog" or "Articles" - aligns with premium positioning. The section presents P-XEL's informed point of view, not generic advice.

## Target audience

- Founders, CEOs, marketing directors at Belgian SMEs (10-200 employees)
- Startup founders evaluating build partners
- Decision-makers comparing agencies, freelancers, and studios
- People actively searching for pricing, subsidies, or technology choices

## Content pillars

### Pillar 1: Cost and investment
- Primary article: Combien coute un site web en 2026
- Supporting: Aides a la digitalisation en Belgique
- Intent: Commercial / Transactional
- Goal: Capture price-shoppers and convert them by showing value

### Pillar 2: Technology choices
- Primary article: No-code, low-code ou full-code
- Supporting: Design system (condensed)
- Intent: Informational / Commercial
- Goal: Position P-XEL as the expert advisor on tech decisions

### Pillar 3: AI and modern workflow
- Primary article: Comment l'IA accelere la creation de votre produit digital (rewritten)
- Intent: Informational / Commercial
- Goal: Differentiate P-XEL as AI-native studio, show speed advantage

## On-page SEO requirements (per article)

1. H1 contains primary keyword
2. H2s contain secondary keywords or semantic variations
3. Meta title: primary keyword + brand (max 60 chars)
4. Meta description: value proposition + CTA (max 155 chars)
5. URL slug: short, keyword-rich, no dates
6. Hero image with descriptive alt text containing keyword
7. Internal links: minimum 2 to service pages, 1 to another article
8. FAQ section with schema markup (minimum 3 questions)
9. CTA block: mid-article + end-article
10. Reading time displayed

## Structured data per article

- Article schema (headline, author, datePublished, dateModified, image)
- FAQ schema (embedded in each article)
- BreadcrumbList schema
- Organization schema (already site-wide)

## Content quality standards

- Minimum 1200 words per article (after rewrite)
- Maximum 2500 words - dense, not padded
- Every section must deliver value or be cut
- No filler paragraphs, no generic advice
- P-XEL opinion or experience in every article
- At least one concrete example, number, or case per article
- Tone: expert, direct, premium. Not academic. Not casual.

## Publishing priority

1. Combien coute un site web en 2026 (money page, publish first)
2. Aides a la digitalisation en Belgique (unique angle, low competition)
3. Comment l'IA accelere votre produit digital (differentiator)
4. No-code, low-code ou full-code (decision-making content)
5. Pourquoi un design system est essentiel (authority builder, lower priority)

Tendances UX/UI 2026 is cut. Does not meet quality or lead-gen standards.

## Conversion strategy per article

Every article must include:
- **Mid-article CTA**: Contextual callout box linking to booking or relevant service page
- **End-article CTA**: Full CTA block with heading + button to book a call
- **Sidebar/sticky element** (desktop): "Discutons de votre projet" with Calendly link
- **Related articles**: 2-3 links to other perspectives articles

## Measurement

Track per article:
- Organic impressions and clicks (Search Console)
- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- CTA click rate
- Calendly bookings attributed (UTM parameters)
