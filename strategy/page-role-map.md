# Page Role Map - P-XEL Studio

Every page has a defined role, a reason to exist, and a clear relationship to conversion. No page is filler.

---

## Role Definitions

| Role | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Experiential** | Fully custom-designed. The page IS the product demonstration. Conversion through quality of experience. |
| **SEO-driven** | Content-rich, structured for search ranking. Funnels organic traffic into the experience layer. |
| **Editorial** | Builds topical authority. Captures informational queries. Nurtures visitors toward commercial intent. |
| **Conversion** | Minimal friction. Exists to capture the decision once it is made. |
| **Legal** | Required by law. No design investment beyond basic readability. |

## Decision Summary

### 1. Pages that remain essential (21)

| Page | Why essential |
|------|--------------|
| Homepage `/` | Core experience. Cannot exist without it. |
| 6 case studies `/work/[slug]/` | Proof of capability. Irreplaceable. |
| Contact `/contact/` | Conversion endpoint. Non-negotiable. |
| 5 service pages `/services/[slug]/` | SEO foundation for commercial keywords. Revenue-driving search entry points. |
| 4 city pages `/[city]/` | Local SEO foundation. Critical for Belgian market. |
| 7 blog posts `/blog/[slug]/` | Topical authority. Informational SEO. Internal linking fuel. |

### 2. Pages merged (2 -> 1)

| Merged from | Merged into | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| `/a-propos/` (about) | `/studio/` | A standalone "about" page is agency convention. Combining story, process, philosophy, and credibility into one `/studio/` page creates a stronger, more cohesive experience. |

### 3. Pages removed (3)

| Removed | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| `/services/` (hub) | Intermediary page with no unique value. The homepage handles the capability overview. Individual service pages are reachable through footer, homepage, city pages, and search. A hub that lists 5 links is a dead end. |
| `/projets/` (hub) | Same problem. A grid of thumbnails is the most generic possible treatment of a portfolio. `/work/` replaces it as a designed experience, not a link directory. |
| `/a-propos/` (standalone) | Absorbed into `/studio/`. See merge above. |

### 4. Pages that stay SEO-driven

| Pages | SEO role |
|-------|---------|
| 5 service pages | National commercial keywords. Highest SEO priority. |
| 4 city pages | Local commercial keywords. Critical for local pack and map queries. |
| 7 blog posts | Informational keywords with commercial undertones. Authority building. |
| Blog index | Light. Organizational. |

These 17 pages carry the SEO weight. They are content-rich, schema-marked, and structured for ranking. Their design is premium but their primary job is search visibility and funneling traffic.

### 5. Pages that stay premium/conversion-driven

| Pages | Conversion role |
|-------|----------------|
| Homepage `/` | Primary. The narrative scroll builds desire and ends with action. |
| Work `/work/` | Secondary. Showcases capability, bridges to case studies. |
| 6 case studies `/work/[slug]/` | Secondary. Deepens trust, proves outcomes, bridges to contact. |
| Studio `/studio/` | Secondary. Builds founder credibility and connection. |
| Contact `/contact/` | Terminal. Captures the decision. |

These 10 pages carry the experience weight. They are fully custom-designed. Their primary job is to make visitors feel the quality and convert through desire, not pressure.

## How the Journey Flows

### First impression to qualified inquiry

```
ARRIVAL (0-3 seconds)
  Visitor lands on any page.
  Immediate impression: this is premium, modern, precise.
  The dark canvas, typography, and visual quality signal top-tier.

UNDERSTANDING (3-15 seconds)
  On homepage: the narrative explains what P-XEL does.
  On service page: the capability is clear and proven.
  On city page: P-XEL is local, credible, relevant.
  On blog post: P-XEL knows this topic deeply.

PROOF (15-45 seconds)
  Visitor encounters case studies (directly or through links).
  They see real work, real decisions, real results.
  The gap between "their current state" and "what P-XEL delivers" becomes tangible.

DESIRE (45-90 seconds)
  The quality of the experience itself creates want.
  "I want my product to feel like this site feels."
  "I want results like Arduenna Gin's."
  "I want someone who ships, not just presents."

DECISION (90+ seconds)
  The visitor has seen enough. The CTA is obvious.
  "Reserver un appel" or "Lancer mon projet."
  The action feels like a natural next step, not a sales funnel.
  -> /contact/ or Calendly
```

### Critical design rule

At no point in this journey should the visitor feel like they are on a standard agency website. The structure supports this by eliminating the patterns that create that feeling: no services hub dropdown, no portfolio thumbnail grid, no "about us" + "our team" + "our process" as separate pages.

The structure is lean. The experience is rich. The conversion is inevitable.
