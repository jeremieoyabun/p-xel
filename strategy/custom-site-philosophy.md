# Custom Site Philosophy - P-XEL Studio

---

## 1. Project Vision (Restated)

P-XEL is not building a website. P-XEL is building a product.

The site itself is the first product P-XEL ships in this new era. It must function as living proof of everything the studio claims: that it can think strategically, design at the highest level, build with modern technology, and deliver something that converts.

This is not a redesign. There is no old site to improve. There is a blank canvas and a clear mandate: build the most compelling digital studio presence in Belgium. A site that makes every prospect think "I want this level for my product" within 3 seconds of landing.

The site must feel like it was designed in 2030 and shipped today. Not bleeding-edge for the sake of novelty. Future-facing because P-XEL builds future-facing products.

### What this site must prove without saying it:

- P-XEL can conceive, design, and build at the highest level
- P-XEL thinks in products, not pages
- P-XEL ships, not just presents
- Working with P-XEL means your product will feel like this
- This is not a freelancer. This is not a generic agency. This is a product design studio with a point of view.

---

## 2. What We Are Not Building

### Not an agency website
Agency websites follow a formula: hero with tagline, services grid, portfolio carousel, about section, contact form. They look interchangeable. They feel like templates even when they are not. We are not building this.

### Not a portfolio dump
Portfolio-first sites work for illustrators and photographers. For a product studio, the portfolio is proof, not the product. The site itself is the product. Case studies support the narrative. They do not define the structure.

### Not an information architecture exercise
Traditional web projects start with a sitemap and fill in content. This project starts with a desired experience and builds the architecture to deliver it. The structure serves the feeling, not the other way around.

### Not a template with custom paint
We are not taking a SaaS landing page pattern (hero, features grid, testimonials, pricing, FAQ) and styling it with violet and dark mode. Every section, every transition, every piece of content exists because it earns its place.

---

## 3. What We Are Building

### A product experience that sells product experiences

The site is a self-referential proof of concept. The way it moves, the way it reveals information, the way it handles interaction, the way it converts: all of this demonstrates P-XEL's capability more convincingly than any case study or service description ever could.

### A narrative, not a brochure

The homepage is not a page. It is a sequence. A visitor enters a story: what P-XEL does, how it thinks, what it has built, why it matters, and what happens next. Each section advances the narrative. No section exists in isolation.

### A conversion instrument

Every design choice, every animation, every word serves one of two goals:
1. Build enough trust and desire that the visitor books a call
2. Move the visitor deeper into the site where goal 1 can happen

Beauty is not separate from conversion. Beauty IS the conversion mechanism. The quality of the experience is the most persuasive argument.

## 4. What Replaces Standard Service-Tier Pages

### The problem with service pages

Traditional service pages (creation-site-web, application-web-mvp, ux-ui-design, etc.) create a flat hierarchy: 5 equal pages, each with the same structure, each competing for attention. They fragment the narrative into silos. They force the visitor to self-categorize before understanding what P-XEL actually does.

Most visitors do not arrive thinking "I need a UI/UX designer." They arrive thinking "I need someone who can build what I have in my head." Service pages answer the wrong question.

### The replacement: Capability Narratives

Instead of isolated service tier pages, the site presents capabilities as an interconnected system:

**Homepage experience flow:**
The homepage handles the heavy lifting. It does not just introduce services. It demonstrates them. Through visual storytelling, interaction design, and structured reveals, the homepage communicates the full scope of what P-XEL delivers.

**Deep-dive pages (not "service pages"):**
When a visitor wants to go deeper, they do not land on a templated service page. They land on a purpose-built experience that shows the capability in action:

- **The "Build" page** (`/build/` or `/services/`) unifies web apps, MVPs, websites, and landing pages as different expressions of the same capability: P-XEL takes an idea and turns it into a shipped digital product. The distinction between "website" and "web app" is relevant for SEO but irrelevant for the visitor's decision. The page shows the spectrum, not the silos.

- **The "Design" page** (`/design/`) covers UI/UX, product design, and design systems as one integrated discipline. Not "we do UX audits" and "we do UI design" on separate pages. One cohesive story about how P-XEL approaches design.

- **The "Brand" page** (`/brand/`) covers branding and identity. This one can stand alone because branding clients often have a distinct entry intent.

### SEO reality check

Service pages still exist for SEO. They carry the right keywords, the right schema, the right metadata. But they are not just SEO pages with copy. They are designed experiences that happen to rank. The content written in `/content/services/` provides the substance. The implementation elevates it into something memorable.

**The resolution:** SEO-targeted URLs exist and are indexable. But the visitor experience is not defined by them. The homepage and the capability narratives handle the experiential layer. Service URLs handle the search entry layer. Both lead to the same conversion point.

## 5. The Homepage as the Core Product

The homepage is not a landing page. It is the experience.

### What the homepage must accomplish:

1. **0-3 seconds:** Visceral impact. The visitor knows this is premium, modern, and different. No processing needed. The feeling is immediate.
2. **3-10 seconds:** Clarity. The visitor understands what P-XEL does. Not through reading, through experiencing. The headline confirms it. The visual language reinforces it.
3. **10-30 seconds:** Credibility. Proof appears naturally: stats, logos, project glimpses. The visitor's internal skeptic is addressed without feeling sold to.
4. **30-60 seconds:** Desire. The visitor sees work, process, and capability that matches or exceeds what they want for their own product. The gap between "their current state" and "what P-XEL can deliver" becomes visceral.
5. **60+ seconds:** Action. The visitor has enough context to act. The CTA is obvious, easy, and feels like the natural next step, not a sales pitch.

### What the homepage does NOT do:

- List every service in a grid
- Show every project in a carousel
- Answer every question on one page
- Compete with service pages for attention
- Feel like it was built from a wireframe template

---

## 6. Case Studies as Experiential Proof

Case studies are not "project pages." They are proof experiences.

Each case study is a mini product demo: showing how P-XEL thought about the problem, what decisions were made, what was built, and what happened after launch. They should feel like opening a product, not reading a PDF.

### Case study experience principles:
- Large-scale visuals that show the actual product, not mockup screenshots
- The story is told through design decisions, not just deliverable lists
- Results are quantified and prominent
- The transition from case study to CTA feels inevitable, not forced
- Each case study subtly demonstrates a different P-XEL capability

---

## 7. The AI-Native Layer

P-XEL integrates AI into products. The site should demonstrate this, not just state it.

### What AI-native means for the site:
- The site itself may use AI features where they add value (intelligent project intake, adaptive content, smart recommendations)
- AI is referenced as a capability woven into the process, not as a marketing buzzword
- The aesthetic avoids AI cliches (no neural networks, no glowing orbs, no "powered by AI" badges)
- AI fluency is demonstrated through sophistication of the experience, not through AI-themed visuals

### What AI-native does NOT mean:
- An "AI Services" page
- Chatbot widget
- Generative art as decoration
- "We use ChatGPT" as a selling point

---

## 8. The Conversion Architecture

### Primary conversion: Book a call (Calendly)
Every pathway through the site leads to this. Not aggressively. Inevitably.

### Secondary conversion: Project intake form
For visitors who want to provide context before talking. Structured, short, smart.

### Tertiary conversion: Stay in orbit
For visitors who are not ready to act: blog content, case studies, social links. Keep them in the P-XEL universe until they are ready.

### Conversion philosophy:
The site does not "sell." It demonstrates capability at such a high level that the visitor's logical conclusion is: "I need to talk to them." The CTA is permission to act on a desire that was already created.

## 9. What This Changes About the Build

### Content layer (already written):
The content in `/content/` remains valid. Homepage copy, service page copy, city page frameworks: this content provides the substance. The philosophy change is about how this content is presented, not what it says.

### Design layer (next):
The design must now be conceived as a custom experience, not a component assembly. Each page is a designed sequence. Components serve the sequence, not the other way around.

### Technical layer (Phase 6):
Next.js architecture must support:
- Page-level transitions and scroll-based reveals
- Dynamic content loading for case studies
- Performance that feels instant (< 1s perceived load)
- SEO requirements met without compromising the experience
- Possible AI-powered features in the intake flow

### What stays the same:
- All SEO strategy, keyword maps, and schema plans
- All local SEO infrastructure
- Brand DNA: violet, black, white, geometric, premium
- Architect Noir direction and design tokens
- Typography: Space Grotesk, Satoshi, JetBrains Mono
- Voice: "on" / "vous" / no "tu" / sharp and clear
- Conversion flow: Calendly primary, form secondary
