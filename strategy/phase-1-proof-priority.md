# Phase 1 Proof Priority - P-XEL Studio

How projects are selected, ranked, and deployed across the site to prove P-XEL's positioning: "We design, build, and ship digital products."

---

## Positioning Test

Every project shown on the site must pass this filter:

1. Does it prove P-XEL designs AND builds? (not just one or the other)
2. Is the output a product, platform, or system? (not a static deliverable)
3. Would a startup founder or PME director see this and think "I want this for my product"?

## Recommendation

### Homepage Featured (3): BillyCheck, ChairSplit, Greenmood

These three stay. They are the strongest proof triangle:
- **BillyCheck**: Own SaaS, AI-powered, full-stack. Proves "we ship our own products."
- **ChairSplit**: Own SaaS, multi-tenant, PWA, Stripe. Proves depth and production-grade work.
- **Greenmood**: Client platform, enterprise scale, configurators. Proves "we handle complex B2B."

### Work Page: 5 projects with revised hierarchy

**Featured (3, full viewport each):** BillyCheck, ChairSplit, Greenmood

**Secondary (2, compact, side by side):** Oyabun, Arduenna

### Hopscotch: Removed from Phase 1

Hopscotch is a 51-page design document delivered in 24 hours. It is impressive as a speed anecdote, but it does not prove "we ship products." It is the only project in the lineup that is not a digital product, platform, or application.

- The speed claim ("51 pages en 24h") is already used as a proof point in the homepage differentiators section (Beat 6, point 03) and in the founder story on the studio page. The anecdote carries its weight without needing a dedicated case study page.
- The Hopscotch case study targets "design document commercial" - a keyword with minimal search volume and no conversion potential.
- Removing Hopscotch tightens the proof set to 5 projects that all demonstrate product thinking and execution.

Hopscotch can return in Phase 2 if a case study for design/art direction services is needed alongside the branding service page.

### Oyabun: Promoted to secondary on Work page

Oyabun replaces Hopscotch in the Phase 1 lineup. It brings:
- **Tech range**: Web3, gaming, Telegram app. Shows P-XEL works in cutting-edge domains.
- **Product credibility**: An actual application, not a design document.
- **Audience appeal**: Startup founders and tech companies see Oyabun and think "they get our world."
- **Visual impact**: Gaming/Web3 aesthetic creates visual variety against the SaaS and B2B projects.

Oyabun is secondary (not featured) because:
- Web3/gaming is a niche audience. Most leads come from SaaS and web app queries.
- The three featured projects cover the core positioning completely. Oyabun adds range, not core proof.

### Arduenna: Stays as secondary

Arduenna proves marketing execution and ROI. The "2 ans de CA en 2 mois" stat is one of P-XEL's strongest numbers. It stays as a secondary project on the work page.
